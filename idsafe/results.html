RECENT CASES

Debt Collector Dismisses Lawsuit

10/24/2014

One of our clients was sued last year for consumer debts arising from an alleged repossessed vehicle. The law firm for the debt collector filed a motion for summary judgment. We entered the case and opposed the motion due to evidentiary problems. The Plaintiff's/Debt Collector's law firm did not reply to our opposition; instead, the hearing on the motion was rescheduled, then unexpectedly taken off calendar by the Plaintiff/Debt Collector. Later, a stipulation for dismissal was unexpectedly sent over by the Plaintiff/Debt Collector, and the case case was dismissed with prejudice. The client paid nothing to the debt collector.



Debt Collector Files a Time-Barred Lawsuit, Then Pays Our Client's Attorney's Fees & Dismisses Suit

4/30/2014

A woman contacted us regarding old credit card debt. She was being sued by a Las Vegas law firm. After some research, it was determined that the attorney had filed the lawsuit past the statute of limitations; in other words, the debt was too old and it was time-barred by statute. We filed a motion to dismiss, and seeking attorneys fees and costs. The the law firm conceded that the lawsuit had been improperly filed and ultimately they agreed to pay the client's attorneys fees and costs and dismiss the case, which made the client very happy. The client paid nothing to the debt collector.



Debt Collector Drops Lawsuit Against Client

11/07/2013

Client was sued for a debt. The amount of the debt exceeded $20,000. After we associated on the case, filed an answer an sent out discovery, the Plaintiff offered to dismiss the suit without payment, each side bearing its own fees and costs. Client paid nothing to the debt collector.



Debt Collector Drops Lawsuit After It Wins the Arbitration

10/30/2014

A woman hired us to represent her in a debt collection case. She had already been sued and had no money to pay due to her medical bills. Before finding me, she had been sued and lost at arbitration. We told here we would do what we could on such late notice. The award exceeded $20,000. We associated in the lawsuit, filed a trial de novo, and sent out the interrogatories and requests for production of documents. Shortly after, the case settled. The client paid nothing to the debt collector.
