Escrow

Escrow by definition is a deposit of funds, a deed or other instrument by one party for the delivery to another party upon completion of a particular condition or event. Lets break this down a bit further.





1. Opening Escrow – what it means when escrow has opened is that the title company has received a signed purchase contract with all the necessary addendums and/or attachemtns along with an earned money deposit (EMD).

2. What goes on during escrow?
a. Escrow company will order a preliminary title report which will be forwarded to the necessary parties to review
b. When applicable the homeowners association will be notified and the appropriate HOA statements will be ordered
c. The sellers mortgage payoff will be ordered
d. Escrow will collect other necessary documentation such termite report, homeowners insurance and deliver necessary disclosures.
e. The HUD 1 will be produced and submitted to lender and agent
f. Once all of the loan documents are received, the escrow officer schedules a signing appointment with the buyer

3. Escrow Signing – All parties will sign necessary documentation prior to the estimated close of escrow date. After this is complete the documents will be sent to the lender for final review, approval, and funding.

4. Funding and recording will take place once the all the lender documents are approved and the file has been funded

5. Funds will then be disbursed and final closing packages will be issued necessary parties to review statements will be orderedinsurance and deliver necessary disclosures.
